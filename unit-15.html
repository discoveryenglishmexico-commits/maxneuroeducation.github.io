🇪🇸 Exploring Spain

From medieval castles to sun-drenched coasts

🇪🇸

Spain

The Alcázar of Segovia

A stone fortification located in the old city of Segovia, Spain. The Alcázar was originally built as a fortress but has served as a royal palace, a state prison, a Royal Artillery College and a military academy since then. The castle is one of the inspirations for Walt Disney's Cinderella Castle.

The Costa del Sol

A region in the south of Spain, in the autonomous community of Andalusia Province of Malaga. Today the region is a tourist destination of world renown.

The Prado

Spain's national museum and one of the best in Europe. Remarkable collection of European paintings.

Park of the Pleasant Retreat

One of the largest parks of the city of Madrid, Spain. The park belonged to the Spanish Monarchy until the late 19th century, when it became a public park. It's a magnificent park, filled with beautiful sculptures and monuments, galleries, a peaceful lake and host to a variety of events.

There are so many noteworthy tourist attractions to visit in Madrid, too bad that the Williams don't have enough time to get to know more about this exciting place, they have to continue with their journey to France, Italy and to wrap it up in Dutchland.

🇮🇹 Ancient Rome Awaits

Gladiators, fountains, and timeless wonders

🇮🇹

Italy

Ostia Antica

One of Rome's underrated gems but one of the best tourist attractions. It contains the strikingly well safeguarded remains of Rome's ancient port. It's fascinating and just dazzling to explore.

The Colosseum

An absolute must for any tour of Rome. In its time it was the largest amphitheatre of the Roman Empire, where gladiators, criminals and lions alike fought for their lives.

The Trevi Fountain

An iconic monument in the city. It was designed and completed in the 18th century. Tourists come to the fountain in order to throw a coin, which is, according to a local legend, supposed to bring good luck.

The Spanish Steps

One of the city's top attractions, the flight of 138 stairs is the biggest in Europe. They are topped by the "Trinita dei Monti" church, and below there is the large "Piazza di Spagna", which is also a major shopping destination in the city, and hosts several designer boutiques, where Mrs. Mary and Kimberly Williams had no trouble doing some good old shopping.

🇩🇪 Berlin's Grandeur

The final stop of the Williams' European tour

🇩🇪

Germany

The Williams in Berlin

Their visit to Dutchland was as exhilarating as the other countries. They started off at the "Reichstag" building, a place in which one can look out from the building's glass dome to get a bird's eye view of the hustle and bustle in the city.

"The Gendarmenmarkt" is one of the most spectacular squares in Berlin, located close to Friedrichstraße, Berlin's exclusive shopping street in the central Mitte district. Three of the most impressive examples of architecture in the capital city are to be found here: the (Konzerthaus) Concert House and the German and French Cathedrals.

The Friedrichstraße is the main point for culture and shopping as a street in central Berlin. This downtown area is known for its stylish landholdings. Place where the Williams relished a lot due to its wide array of emporiums and restaurants. (Shop, shop, shop/chow, chow, chow)

They couldn't pass out on the largest palace in Berlin, and the only surviving royal residence in the city. "Charlottenburg Palace" and its gorgeous gardens are a major blast as a tourist attraction.

To wrap up their trip to the old continent, they had a slight walk through the Berlin's wall memorial and a stroll through the "Brandenburg Gate", Berlin's signature attraction.

📚 Grammar Review: Phrasal Verbs with "Off"

Drop off (Separable)

To take someone, or to deliver something, to a place (usually in a car)

On your way to work, could you drop off the books at the library?
I'm going in the direction of your office, so I can drop you off on the way.

Get off (Inseparable)

To depart for a journey or to leave a place to go somewhere

I want to get off early tomorrow morning so we can get to our destination in the afternoon.
I have to get off to the office now.

Head off / Set off (Inseparable)

To start a journey

Every year in August, we head off to the mountains in Vancouver.
Where are you heading off this summer?

See off (Separable)

To go to the airport, etc. in order to say goodbye to someone
To force someone to leave a place

I am going to the airport to see my husband off.
Don't try to force your way into my house, or I will ask my dogs to see you off.

Take off (Inseparable)

(For airplanes, birds, or insects) To begin to fly

I like watching airplanes take off.
Look at that baby eagle trying to take off!
