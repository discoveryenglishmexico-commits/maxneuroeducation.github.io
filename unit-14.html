The Williams Family is finally at the Runway ready to set off on their voyage to Europe. It's the daybreak and they have enough time to check in at the commercial airline reception. They have plenty of luggage, reason why they dropped by so early. They are certain that if their luggage weighs more than what the airline grants, they are gonna be in for it.

Over at the customs desk the agent in charge asks them to show their passports, itinerary, visas, travel health insurance and their declaration of presence. After he goes over them, he hands them over their boarding cards and luggage tabs.

After they check the departure schedule, they head straight to the terminal departure gate to wait for their flight's announcement; they hope that their departure flight will be on time. 📚 14. Grammar Review

Review the key grammar concepts from this unit.

🔮 Third Conditional

The third conditional portrays an unreal situation related to the past.

Example 1: "If I had known you were coming, I would have cleaned the house."
Example 2: "If I had finished work earlier, I would have gone to the movie."

⏳ Past Perfect Continuous

We use the Past Perfect Continuous to show that something started in the past and continued up until another time in the past. Notice that this is related to the Present Perfect Continuous; however, the duration does not continue until now, it stops before something else in the past.

Examples:
She had been working at that company for three years when it went out of business.
How long had you been waiting to get on the bus?
Mike wanted to sit down because he had been standing all day at work.

🔑 Modal Verbs: Can/Could and Will/Would

Modal Verbs are used for asking for permission, giving advice, ability/inability and possibility/impossibility.

Can:
"You can come to the party if you want."
"Can I leave the office early today?"
"I can speak English."

Could:
"I could swim quite well when I was younger."
"Could you open the window please?"

Will:
"Will you help me?"
"I know it's your turn to cook dinner tonight, but I'll do it."

Would:
"Would you like a cup of tea?"
"If I were you, I would say sorry."
