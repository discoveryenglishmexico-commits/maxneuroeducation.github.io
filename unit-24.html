📚 Grammar Review

Adverbs

Adverbs are an important part of speech. They usually answer questions such as how?, where?, when?, how often? and how much?

What is an Adverb?

An adverb is a word that tells us more about a verb. It "qualifies" or "modifies" a verb (The man ran quickly).

  • John speaks loudly. (How does John speak?)
  • Afterwards she smoked a cigarette. (When did she smoke?)
  • Mary lives locally. (Where does Mary live?)

But adverbs can also modify adjectives (Tara is really beautiful), or even other adverbs (It works very well).

Adverb Form

We make many adverbs by adding -ly to an adjective:

  • quick (adjective) → quickly (adverb)
  • careful (adjective) → carefully (adverb)
  • beautiful (adjective) → beautifully (adverb)

But not all words that end in -ly are adverbs. The words friendly, lovely, lonely and neighborly, for example, are all adjectives.

And some adverbs have no particular form: well, fast, very, never, always, often, still

Comparative and Superlative Adjectives

The comparative form of an adjective is used for comparing two people or things (i.e. he is taller than me), while the superlative is used for comparing one person or thing with every other member of their group (i.e. he was the tallest boy in the class).
