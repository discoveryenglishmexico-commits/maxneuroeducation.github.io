📚 9. Vocabulary Definitions

Learn the meanings of key words from the flight safety instructions.

momentarily – soon; in a moment.
stowed securely – put away safely ("to stow" means to put something away, and "secure" means in a safe place).
overhead compartment – a "compartment" is a small place where you can put things; the "overhead compartments" are the places above the seat where people put their bags.
devices – small machines, like games, mp3 players, DVD players, etc.
exits – where you leave (a plane, room, or building).
mark(ed) – to show where something is (the exits are "marked" with an "exit sign" showing a person walking through a door).
responsibilities – things that you should do or have to do.
emergency – when something very serious happens and you need to act quickly (like a car accident, an earthquake, or a fire).
seat pocket – the "pocket" on the back of the seat in front of you, with magazines, safety instructions, etc.
unable to – cannot; not able to.
loss of cabin pressure – the air in the airplane gets lighter or heavier, and it becomes harder to breathe.
panels – small doors; flat pieces of wood, metal, plastic, etc.
oxygen masks – masks that help you breathe oxygen, for example, when there is a loss of cabin pressure.
adjust – to change the position of something.
as necessary – as you need to.
fasten – to tighten.
federal law – the law of a country.
tampering with, disabling, or destroying – changing, turning off, or breaking.

📚 15. Grammar Review

Review the key grammar concepts from this unit.

🔮 Conditional Forms

Conditional forms are used to imagine events in certain conditions. The conditional can be used to speak about real events that always happen (first conditional), imaginary events (second conditional), or imagined past events (third conditional).

First conditional: If we finish early, we will go out for lunch. (possible situation)
Second conditional: If we had time, we would visit our friends. (imaginary situation)
Third conditional: If we had gone to New York, we would have visited the exhibition. (past imagined situation)

⏰ Present Perfect

The present perfect effectively involves making sure you understand that is always connected in some way to the present moment in time. It covers what happens from a past moment to the present moment in time.

From the past until now: I've lived in New York for twenty years.
Life experience: I've visited every state in the country.
Recent past events that influence the present: I've just had lunch.

🚀 Phrasal Verbs

A phrasal verb is a phrase composed of two or more words, usually a noun used as a verb and a preposition. A combination of a regular verb and a preposition has two meanings, while a phrasal verb has only one.

Look after = take care of
Look down on = think less of
Look into = investigate
Look out = be careful
Look over = examine
Look up to = admire
