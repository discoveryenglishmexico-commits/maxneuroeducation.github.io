📖 1. Reading: The Williams' European Adventure

Read about the Williams family's travel plans.

🌍 A Vacation Abroad

The Williams are planning their trip to foreign countries. They will visit several countries throughout Europe. They sure are eager to get going. First they will check through the internet a travel agency that can offer the best options for them.

They would like to go to Spain, France, Italy and to wrap it up in Dutchland. They're working hard as a whole to obtain their objective. They would like to be able to travel by April of next year on Easter week's vacation and enjoy the spring in Europe.

At the Sunshine Travel Agency the packages are excellent, the flight to Spain is not so expensive and from there they could travel by train to the other places they wish to visit, which makes it even more exciting. Although they plan to check with another agency to make the choice.

Spain, France, Italy, and Dutchland (Netherlands)
By April of next year, on Easter week's vacation
By train

🚀 2. Phrasal Verbs: Traveling

Learn essential phrasal verbs for travel.

Travel Phrasal Verbs

Drop off - to take someone to a place and leave them there
Check in - to register at a hotel
Check out - to leave and pay for your stay at a hotel
Pick up - to go and fetch someone from a place
Set out - to start a journey
Take off - when a plane leaves and begins to fly
Get in - when a plane arrives at an airport
Get away - to leave to go somewhere for a break or holiday
Get on - to get on board
Speed up - to increase speed
Look around - to explore what is near you
Hurry up - to rush and not waste time
Go back - to return to your place
See off - to go to the place where someone is leaving and say goodbye
Look forward - to wait for something anxiously that is going to happen

🛟 7. Flight Safety Instructions

Read the safety instructions carefully.

Safety on Board

"We'll be taking off momentarily, so please make sure your carryon baggage is stowed securely, either in an overhead compartment or under the seat in front of you. Mobile phones and other electronic devices should be turned off.

There are six exits on this plane – 3 doors, on each side. All exits are clearly marked with an exit sign. Please take the time to find the exit nearest you. If you're seated in an exit row, please review the responsibilities for emergency exit seating.

If there is a loss of cabin pressure, the panels above your seat will open, and oxygen masks will drop down. If this happens, place the mask over your nose and mouth, and adjust it as necessary. Be sure to adjust your own mask before helping others.

In the event of a water landing, life vests are under your seat. Whenever the seat belt sign is on, please make sure your seat belt is fastened. Smoking is not allowed on our flights, and federal law prohibits tampering with, disabling, or destroying a smoke detector.

Thank you for your attention. We hope you enjoy your flight!"
