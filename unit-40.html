The renewable energy revolution: Solar, wind, and the future of sustainable power.

📖 Reading: The Renewable Revolution

The 21st Century Energy Shift

Energy in the 21st century is already turning out to be very different from energy in the 20th century. Coal plants that used to provide most of the country's electricity are being shut down by the hundreds and renewable energy is becoming more cost efficient and prevalent day by day.

The tendencies are heavily in favor of renewable energy, and solar energy in particular is taking the U.S.A. and the world by storm.

📊 Energy Statistics (2007-2012):
• Coal: Electricity generation fell 24.9% from 2.02 billion MW-hrs to 1.51 billion MW-hrs
• Wind: Grew 309% to 140.8 million MW-hrs
• Solar: Grew 607% to 4.3 million MW-hrs
• Note: This doesn't include distributed solar on residential rooftops or any installations from 2013, which was a record year for solar, installing about 4.4 GW in the U.S.

Of course, renewable energy from wind and solar is still only about 3.2% of total electricity produced but you can see that the trajectory for renewable energy is only moving higher.

Wind vs. Solar: The Future

While electricity generated from wind is much larger than solar today, it's not the future of renewable installations. Most of the easily and economically developable wind resources in the U.S. have been exploited and costs aren't falling as rapidly as they are in solar.

☀️ The Solar Advantage:
The solar industry is where there's high potential because solar power has the potential to provide enough electricity for the entire country and costs are still falling rapidly.

Utility scale projects are less than half of what they cost in 2010 and residential projects are about one-third cheaper. If we go back even further, the cost of a solar panel 35 years ago was more than $100 per watt and today you can get one for around $0.65 per watt, a cost reduction of more than 99%.

💰 Solar Economics:
• A solar power plant will generate optimal energy about 20%-25% of the time
• Each watt will produce about 1.75-2.19 kW-hrs of electricity each year
• A utility scale project with a 10% return on investment would create electricity for 9.3 to 11.7 cents per kW-hr without subsidy
• That compares to an average price of 9.6 cents per watt overall in the U.S.

The Economic Driver

The driver of coal's declining role in electricity and the growth in renewable energy (particularly solar) is economics. As costs continue to fall, we'll see more growth for renewable energy and you can make money off the trend.

💼 Investment Opportunity:
With all of the trends I've pointed out above, you can see that renewable energy has a bright future. Solar power in particular is an attractive market for investors.

As the industry grows, the future of energy is in these cleaner sources of power that are suddenly economically viable as well. They will dominate energy in the 21st century and that's where you should be looking to put your money down.

Google's Green Initiative

Tech giant Google is investing $1 billion in renewable energies to make its operations totally green. The company hopes to exploit solar energy, wind farms and other forms of alternative energy to power its data centers around the world.

Google still has a long way to go before all of its operations are fuelled by renewable energy. There are lots of challenges in getting there, not the least of which is operating in many jurisdictions that are in different parts of the world.

🌍 Google's Vision:
Google invests in innovative renewable energy projects that have the potential to transform the energy landscape and help provide more clean power to businesses and homes around the world.
